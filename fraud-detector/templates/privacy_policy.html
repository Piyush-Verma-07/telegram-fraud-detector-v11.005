Privacy Policy

Effective Date: 25 May 2026

Welcome to Fraud Detector. This Privacy Policy explains how Fraud Detector handles information when you use our website and application services.

This Privacy Policy applies to:

  • The Fraud Detector web application
  • The Fraud Detector Android application
  • Official website: https://frauddetector.in

1. Information We Process

Fraud Detector allows users to analyze URLs, domains, messages, and suspicious text content.

The information submitted by users is processed temporarily for fraud and phishing analysis purposes.

  • We do NOT require account creation
  • We do NOT provide user login systems
  • We do NOT permanently store submitted scan content
  • We do NOT sell user data

2. Third-Party Security & Threat Intelligence Services

To provide fraud detection and cybersecurity analysis, Fraud Detector may interact with third-party security and reputation services.

These services may include:

  • Google Safe Browsing API
  • AbuseIPDB
  • URLHaus
  • Domain reputation and domain age services
  • Threat intelligence datasets

Submitted URLs or related indicators may be checked against these services to help determine potential phishing, scam, malware, or malicious activity.

3. Local Threat Intelligence Datasets

Fraud Detector also uses locally maintained and manually updated cybersecurity datasets, including but not limited to:

  • OpenPhish
  • ThreatFox
  • URLHaus datasets
  • Scam pattern databases
  • Trusted domain datasets

These datasets are used only for detection and comparison purposes.

4. Cookies and Temporary Technical Data

Fraud Detector may use limited browser storage technologies or temporary session mechanisms required for:

  • Basic application functionality
  • Performance optimization
  • PWA (Progressive Web App) support
  • Security and session stability

We do not currently use advertising cookies or behavioral tracking systems.

5. Analytics

Fraud Detector currently does NOT use:

  • Google Analytics
  • Microsoft Clarity
  • Advertising trackers
  • Behavioral analytics systems

Detection results may not always be accurate, may produce false positives or false negatives, and should not be considered guaranteed security advice.

Users should independently verify important decisions involving cybersecurity, payments, accounts, or sensitive information.
